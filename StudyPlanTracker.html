Phase-wise Roadmap
📖
Phase 1 — Foundation (Weeks 1–4)
Cover all concepts: Number system, percentages, grammar fundamentals, syllogism, banking basics. Build a personal formula sheet. Do 15–20 practice Qs per topic before moving on.
🎯
Phase 2 — Practice (Weeks 5–10)
Speed + accuracy drills. 1 sectional test/day. Current affairs daily. Focus on Time & Work, DI, Error Spotting, Puzzles. Target 80%+ sectional accuracy and sub-25 sec/Q speed.
🚀
Phase 3 — Mock & Revision (Weeks 11+)
2 full mocks/week. Deep error analysis after each mock. Revise GA notes daily (15 min). PYQs from last 5 years. Cut weak topics to minimum viable coverage and maximise strengths.

Exam Strategy & Study Tips
📕
English — Priority #1 (Weak Area)
Learn 10 vocab words daily using the word-root method. Attempt 1 RC passage every morning. Master error spotting through grammar rules — not gut feel. Use Arihant English + The Hindu editorials daily.
🔢
Quant — Priority #2 (Weak Area)
Build a chapter-wise formula sheet from day 1. Solve 25–30 Qs per topic before moving on. For RBI: focus on Percentages, SI/CI, Time-Work, DI. For SSC CGL add Geometry, Trigonometry, and Algebra.
🏦
GA / Banking — Priority #3 (Weak Area)
Read one economy/banking news daily (PIB, The Hindu). Use GKToday or Oliveboard for daily CA. Make a one-page GA summary every Sunday. Memorise top RBI, SEBI, IRDAI, NABARD data points.
🧩
Reasoning — Maintain Your Edge
Reasoning is your scoring safety net — protect it. Do 1 puzzle set daily (10 min). Focus on seating arrangements, syllogism, and inequalities. These are top-frequency in both RBI and SSC CGL.
⏰
Daily 4–5 Hour Routine
7–8am: English (60 min) · 9–10am: Quant (60 min) · 11–11:55am: GA/Banking (55 min) · 2–3pm: Reasoning (60 min) · 4–4:30pm: Revision + Vocab (30 min) · Evening: CA news (15 min)
⏱
Exam Time Strategy
RBI Prelims: 100 Qs in 60 min — 36 sec/Q max. SSC CGL Tier-1: 100 Qs in 60 min. Attempt order: Reasoning → GA → English → Quant. Skip and return. Never spend more than 45 sec on any question.
🧠
Spaced Repetition & Memory
Review notes at Day 1 → Day 3 → Day 7 → Day 14. Keep a "mistake journal" from mock tests and re-attempt those Qs every Sunday. Teach concepts aloud — it reveals hidden gaps faster than re-reading.
💻
Best Free Resources
Mocks: Oliveboard, Testbook, PracticeMock · CA: GKToday, Bankersadda · Quant/Reasoning: Rakesh Yadav, Adda247 (YouTube) · English: The Hindu editorial + Gradeup tests · GA: PIB daily digest
